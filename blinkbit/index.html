BlinkBit Browser Extension

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how BlinkBit handles information when you create, preview, save, and export small monochrome pixel animations inside the browser.

Effective date: July 14, 2026 Last updated: July 14, 2026
Privacy summary BlinkBit does not collect personal information, does not track browsing activity, does not display advertising, and does not send animation frames or exported work to external servers. Animation data and preferences are stored locally in the browser.

1. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to the BlinkBit browser extension and the tools provided through its extension interface. It explains how BlinkBit processes animation frames, local preferences, preview activity, and export actions.

BlinkBit is a compact creative studio for building tiny 4-frame monochrome animations on a 4×4 pixel grid. Users can draw one frame at a time, preview the resulting loop, refine transitions, and export finished micro-animations.

This policy does not apply to the browser, browser-extension marketplace, operating system, file manager, or third-party services that a user may choose to use after exporting an animation.

2. How BlinkBit Works

BlinkBit runs inside the browser as a self-contained extension. When the user opens the extension, the interface presents a small pixel grid, four animation frames, playback controls, and export options.

Drawing actions, frame changes, loop playback, and export preparation are processed locally in the browser. BlinkBit does not need to inspect unrelated webpages, access browsing history, or transmit project data to a remote server in order to provide its declared features.

The extension does not require an account, login, subscription, or cloud workspace. Users can create and manage animations without providing identifying information.

3. Information BlinkBit Does Not Collect

BlinkBit does not collect, transmit, or maintain personal information. It is not designed to identify users or associate animation projects with a real person.

In particular, BlinkBit does not collect:

  • Names, email addresses, telephone numbers, or account credentials;
  • Passwords, payment information, or financial details;
  • Browsing history, visited URLs, bookmarks, or search queries;
  • Website content, form entries, private messages, or unrelated files;
  • IP addresses, precise location, device identifiers, or demographics;
  • Advertising identifiers, telemetry, analytics, or behavioral profiles;
  • Remote copies of animation frames, preview histories, or exported projects.

Because these categories of information are not collected, the developer does not maintain a database of identifiable users or a remote archive of BlinkBit projects.

4. Animation Frames and Local Projects

The pixels placed by the user, the four animation frames, and the order in which those frames are played are processed only to create and preview the current micro-animation.

BlinkBit does not upload frame contents, inspect animations for advertising purposes, analyze artistic choices, or use project data to build a user profile.

Locally saved projects remain under the user's control. The extension does not publish, share, or synchronize them automatically.

5. Local Storage and User Preferences

BlinkBit uses the browser's storage capability to save unfinished animations, favorite micro-animations, frame data, and related preferences so that projects can remain available between sessions.

Why the storage permission is required The storage permission allows BlinkBit to preserve animation frames and local settings. It is not used for tracking browsing activity, advertising, profiling, or collecting personal information.

Stored animation data remains in the browser's extension storage. It is not transmitted to the developer, sold to third parties, or used for marketing.

Users can remove locally stored projects and settings by deleting them inside the extension, clearing extension data in the browser, or uninstalling BlinkBit.

6. Preview and Playback

BlinkBit's instant preview feature plays the current four-frame sequence in a loop so the user can evaluate motion and timing. Preview activity is processed locally and is not recorded as analytics.

The extension does not create playback histories, measure engagement, track which animations are previewed most often, or send preview behavior to an external service.

7. Exported Animations

When a user exports an animation, BlinkBit prepares the output locally and makes it available for the user to save or use elsewhere.

Exported files are not automatically uploaded, shared, or published by BlinkBit. After export, any storage, transmission, or sharing performed through another service is governed by that service's own privacy terms.

BlinkBit does not retain a remote copy of exported animations and does not create an export history on a developer-operated server.

8. Browser Permissions

BlinkBit requests only the browser permission needed to preserve local projects and settings. It does not require broad access to browsing history, bookmarks, passwords, tabs, or website content for its core functionality.

storage

The storage permission is used to save animation frames, unfinished projects, favorite projects, and local configuration options. The permission is not used to transmit information to an external service.

9. Advertising, Analytics, and Tracking

BlinkBit does not display advertisements and does not contain advertising SDKs, sponsored-content systems, affiliate tracking, or monetization code based on user data.

The extension does not use analytics services, tracking pixels, browser fingerprinting, session replay tools, or cross-site identifiers.

BlinkBit does not follow users across websites, create interest profiles, or share project activity with advertisers, analytics providers, or data brokers.

10. Remote Code and External Services

BlinkBit does not use remote code. The executable logic and visual assets required for drawing, previewing, and exporting animations are included in the installed extension package.

The extension does not download additional executable scripts from an external server at runtime and does not depend on third-party processing services to create animations.

The browser or extension marketplace may independently process information related to installation, updates, security, or store usage under its own privacy policy.

11. Data Sharing, Sale, and Disclosure

BlinkBit does not sell, rent, license, trade, or disclose personal information. It does not share local projects, animation frames, or preferences with advertisers, analytics companies, data brokers, social networks, or marketing services.

Because BlinkBit does not collect personal information or maintain remote project archives, there is ordinarily no user data available for commercial sharing or disclosure.

12. Data Security and Retention

BlinkBit reduces privacy risk by avoiding the collection and transmission of personal information. Animation data and preferences are stored locally in the browser.

Local projects remain available until they are deleted by the user, cleared through browser settings, removed during a reset, or deleted when the extension is uninstalled.

No technical system can eliminate every possible security risk. However, BlinkBit follows a limited-purpose design and does not maintain a developer-controlled archive of user projects or identifiable information.

13. Children's Privacy

BlinkBit does not knowingly collect personal information from children or from users of any age. The extension does not provide account registration, messaging, targeted advertising, or social networking features.

14. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be revised if BlinkBit's features, permissions, or information practices change. Any updated version will include a revised effective date and will describe the extension's current behavior.

If a future version introduces cloud synchronization, analytics, external processing, or additional permissions, this policy will be updated accordingly.

15. Contact

Questions, privacy inquiries, or requests for clarification may be sent to the support address listed on BlinkBit's official browser-extension store page.
